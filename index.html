Adam Gordon

Software Engineer

I’m a highly motivated junior Software Developer who places the clients' needs and user experience at the forefront of every project I undertake. Having years of experience as a user of assistive technology and having worked closely with developers in a number of my previous roles, my natural instinct is to put myself in the shoes of the client and the end user. I have a passion for solving problems, and building tools that will make a difference to people and believe that this combination of enthusiasm and empathy make me a valuable asset to future employers, colleagues and clients.

The four passions which underpin my approach to software development:

Accessibility

My recent work history has been very much focused on inclusion and accessibility in the digital world. I’m deeply passionate about making sure I do everything within my ability to enable the widest possible user group are able to interact with projects I work on.

Functionality

One of the main concepts that I keep in my head when developing projects is what would it be like for the person who’s going to ultimately use this. Nice design is important, bells and whistles can uplevel a project but it’s all for nothing if the product won’t do what the user needs it to do in the way they need it done.

Engagement

I believe that every project should be engaging for the end user. A site or application might be a pleasure to engage with because it simply saves time or makes a task easier. Other projects can be made engaging because they are attractive and slick and yet others might just have interesting elements or be outright fun to play with.

Efficiency

I work tirelessly to develop my skills so that as well as being able to undertake increasingly complex projects, I can become more and more efficient. I work hard to increase my speed on projects but also to ensure I write the most efficient, semantically clean code I’m able to.
